History of Durga Puja

Durga Puja is not just a festival — it’s the heartbeat of Bengal. It celebrates the goddess Durga’s triumph over the demon Mahishasura, symbolizing the eternal battle between good and evil. While its mythological roots go back to ancient Hindu scriptures, the social and cultural celebration began in full force during the **16th century**.

The earliest organized Durga Puja is believed to have been held in the **royal household of Nadia district**. However, it was during the **British Raj** that Durga Puja took on the community (Barowari) form — organized by local citizens pooling resources. The **first Barowari Puja** was reportedly held in **1909** in Kolkata by Sanatan Dharmotsahini Sabha.

Today, Durga Puja blends tradition with art, music, politics, and global awareness. Kolkata alone hosts over **3,000 pandals**, drawing millions of visitors. The festival ends with **Vijaya Dashami**, where idols are immersed with chants, dance, and tears — bidding the goddess goodbye with hopes of her return next year.
